Mobile App Design

Facilitate further learning and adoption of eco-friendly practices that empower you to help save the world.







restore

Recycle? Compost? Landfill? We created an app, Restore, to understand the motivating factors behind why people do or don’t practice eco-friendly habits and encourage and empower users to develop environmentally conscious habits by providing them with the tools to do so.




Problem

A hopeful environmentalist needs to have access to reliable and consistent information regarding eco-friendly best practices because they have trouble implementing correct habits around reducing, reusing, and recycling. Many users found it diffuclt to access reliable information regarding eco-friendly habits and often were unsure of where to even begin.



Solution

The project was done by my small cohort at The University of Texas at Austin. After talking with people who consider themselves to be non-recyclers or frequent recyclers, we noticed the difficulty to find accurate information regarding recycling, composting and related information. We conducted some initial research via competitor analysis and found a number of products that offered similar solutions, however none combined the informative factor along with gamification and the social components we created in our application. From there the goal was to create a product that was informative and helpful to the user, by providing them with relevant information, while also adding in a gamification aspect that encouraged the user to return to the app and continue making eco-friendly choices.

Research


User Interview and Survey

After understanding the problem and creating our proto-persona, we began our research by conducting 10 different users- 5 who identify as non-recyclers, and 5 who identify as somewhat eco-friendly. We asked each interviewee 17 open ended questions regarding their eco-friendly habits as well as their general knowledge when it comes to environmental best practices and the impact these practices have on the world. From there we sent out a survey with a variety of types of questions surrounding the same subjects of questions asked in our interviews. We received 43 responses and found that nearly 50% of people do not feel like they are doing enough when it comes to reducing, reusing, and recycling, however 75% of people do wish they were doing more.




Competitor Analysis

When conducting a competitive analysis, we found a number of products that offer similar solutions of varying degrees. Most applications we looked at offered information on the how to, while some offered a gamification option that turned recycling into a competition. However, we did not find an app that would offer exactly what we are looking to do- creating a platform that incorporates the informational aspect, as well as the gamification piece into one product.


User Personas


After reviewing our survey and interview data, we realized that we likely have two users: expert and non-expert. Each person will have a different level of understanding when it comes to environmental best practices, therefore we created two user personas to reflect people with more or less knowledge on the subject. We discussed each user in great detail, understanding the expert and non-expert’s goals and limitations well. Our expert, Kristine, already has a plethora of knowledge regarding eco-friendly habits. Her goal is to create a better planet for her children to live in. She is currently doing her best to recycle, reduce and reuses where she can and feels as though she has a good understanding of the protocols for each. However, she wants to do more and would like a product that allows her to access information so she can learn more as well as help educate others on the environment. Our second user, James, is less environmentally friendly. Although he understands why we should reduce, reuse, or recycle, he doesn’t see the impact and does not feel it matters very much. James would like to be more informed and create eco-friendly habits, however he needs a tool that is easy to understand and use so he can begin his environmental journey.






Product Structure

Storyboard & User Flow


When creating our user flow we wanted to make sure the process was easy to follow and eliminate extraneous steps that may be unnecessary. We also wanted to combine the plight of both users, Kristine and James, so the app is easy for everyone to navigate and contains relevant information. Based on the users needs, we wanted to include steps that allow the user to quickly search or scan items to understand how they should dispose of the item. From there, we wanted to include a gaming aspect as a way to encourage repeat use of the app and empower users to invite friends to download the app as well. We added in a gaming and points feature that allows the user to gain points each time they perform an eco-friendly task. We also incorporated a leaderboard function to allow users to see where they stand against their friends as a way to encourage them to increase their habits and use the app more. Another top priority feature was the feed on the homepage. This feature was created to allow users to see their recent activity, including articles they may have read or other information they have recently looked at. The user can quickly reference articles and information via the app if they would like to learn more about a particular subject.




Design

Wireframes & Usability Testing


When creating our wireframes we split into two groups to create two different sets of frames. Group A created wireframes focused on the gaming aspect, while Group B created wireframes focusing on the feed aspect. We used the same onboarding screens and otherwise kept the flow identical. After conducting 5 user tests on each prototype we discussed any pain points and positives within each group. We found that users enjoyed the gaming aspect and were slightly confused by the feed and the way it was displayed. However, users greatly appreciated having the ability to text search an item or scan or take a photo of an item to them learn more about how to dispose of it properly. Group As users also enjoyed the gaming piece and found it fun to compete with their friends via the app. However, we did find that many users were slightly confused by the language used in each version of the app and the icons were difficult to understand as well.




High-Fidelity Wireframes


In our latest iteration of the prototype we converged Group A and Group B’s wireframes to create a prototype that had the gamification piece and a feed for the user to reference. We also updated the icons to make them more understandable and changed the language in certain places so it was easier to understand.




Reflection and Next Steps


After completing this project we have learned how critical user testing and iteration is to a product. Understanding the user’s needs is incredibly important, and as we learned, there are many different types of users with very specific wants and needs. It was very interesting to talk to users and understand their varying levels of understanding when it comes to reducing, reusing and recycling. After gathering our data we understood that our product would need to fit each person’s knowledge level and must be inclusive of every user. We then created our two prototypes to A/B test them on different users, not knowing which platform would be understood or liked more. However, we saw that users appreciated having an easy to use app that provided them with useful information when they needed it. We then iterated on our designs to create our final prototype. One of our biggest difficulties was trying to fit all of our ideas into one product without overwhelming the user. We are very proud of our final prototype, although we would love to iterate on it more and create a useful tool for every user, regardless of their environmental savviness.
